Tim Burton

Tim Burton is a lawyer with 30 years' experience gained in both law firms and industry. He was formerly a partner and member of the senior management team at Pinsent Masons. He now practises as an executive coach and consultant.

During his career, Tim has:

  • Led a City Firm practice group through major strategic change and re-established it as a thriving, profitable business
  • Created and led the in-house legal function of a leading international insurance intermediary
  • Developed a leading practice in the field of insurance & reinsurance
  • Managed a City firm branch office and built up the team selected by Legal Business as ‘Insurance and Reinsurance Dream Team’
  • Coached and mentored professionals in senior in-house and private practice roles
  • Qualified as an executive coach and leadership mentor
  • Worked for a broad range of law firms from a two partner Legal Aid practice to a ‘Top Ten’ City firm
  • Presented at conferences and seminars both at home and abroad.

Less relevantly, but quite interestingly, Tim has also:

  • Conducted the defence of the leading case in the notorious Lloyd’s ‘long tail’ litigation
  • Represented many alleged criminals, including one accused of being involved in the largest ever UK cannabis importation
  • Written Time Bomb, a play performed at the 2008 Edinburgh Festival Fringe

Professional Training & Qualifications

2009: ILM (Institute of Leadership and Management) post-graduate Diploma in Leadership Mentoring and Executive Coaching

2007: Management Futures Coaching Skills Programme – ILM accredited

2004: Trained and accredited as ADR Group mediator

1998: Trained and accredited as CEDR mediator

1982: Admitted as Solicitor, Law Society of England & Wales
